Lahore – City of Gardens

Lahore, the cultural capital of Pakistan, is famed for its Mughal architecture, vibrant bazaars, and rich culinary heritage.

Top Attractions

Badshahi Mosque

The Badshahi Mosque is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the largest mosques in the world. It was built in 1673 by Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb and is known for its beautiful architecture and intricate details.

Shalimar Gardens

The Shalimar Gardens are a UNESCO World Heritage Site built in 1641 by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan. The terraced gardens feature marble pavilions, fountains, and flowing water channels.

Lahore Fort (Shahi Qila)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Lahore Fort spans over 20 hectares and contains palaces, mosques, and gardens dating back to Akbar's reign.

Food Street – Gawalmandi

Famous for traditional Lahori dishes: nihari, paye, and seekh kebabs. An unmissable culinary experience for any visitor.
